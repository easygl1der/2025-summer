- $W$ is the **Weyl group** which is generated by the reflections in $\mathrm{GL}(E)$.
- $\ell:W\to \mathbb{Z}_{\geq0}$ denotes the standard length function. 
	- If $w=s_{i_1}s_{i_2}\dots s_{i_{r}}$ is a reduced expression, i.e. the minimal possible number of simple reflections, then $\ell(w)=r$.
	- How can we reduce the expression? 
		- $s_i^{2}=e$.
		- Braid relations: $(s_is_j)^{m_{ij}}=e$ for some $m_{ij}\geq2$.
			- Origin of $m_{ij}$: it is related to the geometry of the root system associated with the Weyl group, determined by the angle between the simple roots $\alpha _i$ and $\alpha _j$, denoted by $\theta$; then $m_{ij}=\pi/\theta$. 
			- Possible values for $m_{ij}$: $2,3,4,6$.
