According to an elementary theorem in Euclidean geometry, the three angles of a triangle in the plane add up to $180\degree$. This simple fact has a beautiful generalization to any polygon on a Riemannian 2-manifold.

The Gauss-Bonnet theorem states that for an oriented Riemannian 2-manifold $M$, the total Gaussian curvature $\int_M K$ vol is equal to $2\pi$ times the Euler characteristic $\chi(M)$. A key insight is that while Gaussian curvature $K$ depends on the Riemannian metric, its integral and the Euler characteristic do not. This implies the Euler characteristic is independent of the polygonal decomposition of the surface.
