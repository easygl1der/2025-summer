### Projective space
> [!def] projective space
> Given a $k$ -vector space of dimension $n+1$, the **projective space** $\mathbf{P}(E)$ is the quotient of $E-\{ 0 \}$, by the relation $\mathcal{R}: x\mathcal{R}y\iff \exists\lambda\in k^{*}, y=\lambda x$. 

![[1-Daniel-Perrin-2025073019.png]]
> [!remark]
> When $m=0$, we call it a point; when $m=1,2,\dots$, we call it a line.

Let $V\subset \mathbf{P}^{n}$ be a projective algebraic set and let $I_{p}(V)$ be its ideal. Since $I_{p}(V)$ is homogeneous, the quotient ring 
$$
\Gamma_{h}(V)=\mathbf{k}[X_1,\dots,X_n]/I_{p}(V)
$$
is graded. 

> [!remark]
> Unlike affine case, $\Gamma_{h}(V)$ does not define functions on $V$.
