## Sheaf

![[sheaf-and-scheme-2025070700.png]]

Let $M$ be a smooth manifld. Then for each open set $U\subseteq M$, we have $C(U)$. Under point-wise addition and multiplucation, this is a ring. If $V\subseteq U$ then we have the restriction homomorphism $C(U)\to C(V)$ given by actually restricting functions. It's easy to verify that this in fact a sheaf. 

The same for $C^{\infty}$ over $M$. In fact, it's a subsheaf of $C$ over $M$.
